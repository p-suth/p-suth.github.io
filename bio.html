I am a linguist specializing in endangered languages, a photographer, and an author based in Philadelphia, US.

My vocations are rooted in a strong desire to be of service to communities with whom I feel an affinity, who are fighting against the oppressive and homogenizing forces of globalization, neo-liberalism and colonialism, be they ethnic groups, grassroots movements or underground arts communities.

Raised in London and attended the University of Glasgow for a BSc in Geography, it wasn't until trips to Iceland beginning in 2009 that my dual interests in languages and photography arose. Over the next few years I became involved in the polyglot community, resided in Iceland, traveled the world with my wife and completed an MA in Language Documentation & Description. I moved to the US in 2016 where I continued my work with endangered language communities and concert photography.

My other main passion is music, to which I have applied and honed my skills as a photographer and author, building up relationships with the global experimental rock community through work with independent music journalism outlets, including as a founding member of the renowned Fecking Bahamas magazine.
